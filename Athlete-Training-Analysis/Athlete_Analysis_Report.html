Introduction

This project compares athletes training fast-twitch muscle fibers (sprinting, resistance training, plyometrics) versus those training slow-twitch fibers (jogging, light running). We analyze speed, endurance, and recovery time using a simulated dataset inspired by real-world studies.

Data Overview

The dataset includes 100 athletes with the following variables:

  • Training_Type: Fast-Twitch or Slow-Twitch
  • Age: 18â40 years
  • STMF_Percentage: Slow-twitch muscle fiber percentage
  • Speed_mps: Speed in meters per second (100m sprint)
  • Endurance_min: Endurance in minutes (sustained jogging)
  • Recovery_Time_min: Recovery time after high-intensity effort
  • Training_Frequency: Weekly training sessions

Key Findings

Fast-twitch athletes have higher speeds (8â10 m/s) compared to slow-twitch athletes (5â6.7 m/s).

Slow-twitch athletes excel in endurance (60â90 minutes) compared to fast-twitch athletes (30â50 minutes).

Fast-twitch athletes take longer to recover (40â60 minutes) than slow-twitch athletes (20â40 minutes).

Conclusion

Training focus significantly impacts athlete performance. Fast-twitch training enhances speed, while slow-twitch training boosts endurance. These insights can guide training programs for athletes based on their goals.
